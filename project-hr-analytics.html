Objective: Identify which departments face the highest resignation risk, whether high-performing employees are also experiencing burnout, and where regional and pay equity issues are creating additional retention problems.

Key Finding: Operations and Customer Service face the highest resignation risk. The issue is not poor performance; employees in these departments work the most overtime while receiving the lowest bonus rewards. One in four high performers (score ≥85) is also at high risk of burnout, with most of these employees coming from Operations. Lankaran records the highest overtime levels and the strongest performance scores, yet has the lowest bonus rate. This mismatch appears to be a major driver of the region’s 38% resignation risk.

Question 1 — Attrition Risk

High Resignation Risk by Department

Operations and Customer Service lead in resignation risk, separated by only 1 percentage point. Both have high average overtime (21.25 and 21.22 hours) and very low satisfaction scores (2.94 and 2.81). Despite strong performance averages, they also have the lowest bonus rates in the company — Operations at just 41%. That combination is what explains the risk.

Fig 02 — Overtime vs Satisfaction Score
  • 01High overtime alone does not cause attrition — the missing bonus does. Both departments work the most hours and have the highest performance averages, but the lowest bonus rates. Their employees are the hardest working and the least financially rewarded. If this continues, resignations in these departments will keep rising.
  • 02Operations also has the second-highest burnout risk (31%), which sits inside the same group that already has the highest resignation risk. These are the same people — overworked, low-bonused, and burning out. The risks are not separate; they are compounding.
  • 03The pay fairness problem is most visible when comparing Operations and HR. HR has the lowest performance average of all departments, yet it receives a higher bonus rate (46%) than Operations (41%). A high-performing department being compensated less than a lower-performing one will inevitably hurt motivation and increase resignation intent.

Question 2 — Internal Contradiction

High Performance + High Burnout: The Danger Zone

There are 28 employees who have both a high performance score (≥85) and a high burnout risk. 7 of them — 25% — are from Operations, which is the highest share of any department. Since Operations already has the highest overtime average and performance average, having the most burnout among top performers is not a coincidence. But it increases resignation risk even further.

Fig 03 — High-Burnout Employees Among Top Performers (≥85), by Dept
  • 01Operations is the most affected: 7 of the 28 high-burnout top performers work there, making up 15% of all Operations employees. High performance and high overtime go together in this department, and burnout is the natural result of that combination.
  • 02This is not just a wellbeing issue — it is a retention crisis waiting to happen. High-performing employees rarely stay long when they are burning out. Losing them means losing the people who are carrying the most of the department's output.

Question 3 — Absenteeism Pattern

Absence Days, Manager Ratings & Team Fit

The departments with the most absences — IT (58%), Finance (58%), and HR (56%) — also have mid-to-high manager ratings and team compatibility scores. The departments with the fewest absences — Operations (47%) and Customer Service (46%) — have the lowest manager ratings in the company. This is counterintuitive and worth examining carefully.

Question 5 — Regional Risk

Overtime & Resignation Risk by Region

Lankaran has the highest overtime (23.22 hours), the highest average performance score, and a 38% resignation risk — but the lowest bonus rate (36%) of any region. Ganja is the opposite: lowest performance, least overtime, highest salary, and a relatively high bonus rate (51% — second highest). This imbalance is creating a motivation problem in Lankaran. Sumgayit has a 39% resignation risk despite mid-range performance and overtime, and receives the lowest average salary of any region.

Risk Assessment

⚠ Key Risks

⚠ Operations and Customer Service: high-performers leaving first
These are the hardest-working employees in the company, with the highest performance averages, but the fewest bonuses. Operations also has the most burnout among top performers (15% of the department). If this continues, the company risks losing its best people from its most productive departments.
⚠ Manager ratings are misaligned with actual departmental performance
Operations and Customer Service have the highest performance and lowest absenteeism but the worst manager ratings. Departments with lower performance and higher absenteeism (Finance, HR) receive better manager evaluations. This is creating a fairness perception problem — especially in Operations, where burnout and resignation risk are already highest.
⚠ Regional pay inequality is an active retention problem
Lankaran employees outperform and outwork Ganja employees but are paid less and receive fewer bonuses. Sumgayit has the lowest salary despite average output. The overall satisfaction ceiling across regions is 3.45/5 — the entire workforce is undercontented, not just isolated pockets.

Action Items

✓ Recommendations

→ Increase bonus rates for Operations and Customer Service
These are the highest-performing departments with the lowest bonus rates. Operations is at 41% — the lowest in the company. Raising bonus rates to reflect actual contribution is the most direct way to reduce resignation risk here. Employees who work the most and perform the best should not be receiving the least.
→ Address the manager rating problem in Operations and Customer Service
Manager ratings of 2.79 and 2.45 are too low for departments that carry the highest performance load. Since project count is partially driving manager scores in other departments, the evaluation criteria should account for performance output, not just project volume. The Operations department is already at high burnout and resignation risk — management quality matters here more than anywhere else.
→ Review Lankaran and Sumgayit compensation
Lankaran's employees have the best performance scores and the most overtime, but the lowest bonus rate. Sumgayit has the lowest salary with average performance and overtime. Both regions have high resignation risk as a direct result. Increasing salaries in Lankaran and Sumgayit — even moderately — would directly reduce the motivation gap that is driving resignations there.
