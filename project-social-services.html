Objective: Build a unified data model from 5 source tables, compute KPIs, and analyse 420 social service applications by service type, department load, completion rates, risk levels, and geographic distribution across Azerbaijan.

Key Finding: Only 41% of applications are completed. 25% are flagged as high-risk. Processing times vary significantly by department — Psychological Services takes 6.69 days on average vs Finance at 5.22 days. Sumgayit (81 applications) and Baku (90) carry disproportionately high loads with similar staffing.

Risk Assessment

⚠ Key Risks

⚠ 59% of cases are not completed
With only 41% completion, a large portion of citizens who applied for social services are not receiving them — either due to refusal (8%), still in-progress cases (26%), or systemic delays. This is a direct equity and service delivery failure.
⚠ Psychological Services bottleneck
Longest processing time + lowest completion rate + high refusal rate: this department is under critical strain. Failing to address this risks further deterioration as demand for psychological support grows post-pandemic.
⚠ Baku and Sumgayit volume concentration
Baku (90) and Sumgayit (81) together account for 40.7% of all applications. If staffing is evenly distributed across cities, these two offices are significantly understaffed relative to their demand.

Action Items

✓ Recommendations

→ Reallocate staff to Psychological Services and Baku/Sumgayit offices
Model the required staff count based on current caseloads and target a 5.5-day maximum processing time for all departments. Staff reallocation is the fastest lever available without structural changes.
→ Set completion rate targets by service type
The current 41% average is too broad to drive action. Set differentiated targets: Social Assistance ≥50%, Psychological Support ≥40%, Disability Support ≥45%. Track monthly to identify which departments are improving.
→ Fast-track high-risk (priority) applications
25% of applications are flagged high-risk. Create a separate processing queue for these cases with a 3-day target completion window. A dedicated high-risk lane exists in most well-functioning case management systems — this should be adopted here.
